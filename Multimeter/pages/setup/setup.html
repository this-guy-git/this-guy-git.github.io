Setup: Hardware & Software

Hardware Components

Get the parts below to build your multimeter:

  • 1 × Arduino UNO R3/R2/R1
  • Pushbuttons × 3 (standard momentary switches)
  • 1 kΩ resistor × 3
  • 100 kΩ resistor × 1
  • 18 kΩ resistor × 1
  • 220 Ω resistor × 1
  • 100 Ω resistor × 1
  • 10 kΩ resistor × 1
  • 2 × Diodes (e.g., 1N4007 or similar)
  • 1 × LCD 16×2 character display
  • 1 × p-channel MOSFET transistor (PMOS), any logic level PMOS will work!
  • 1 × USB cable to connect Arduino to your computer
  • 1 × Breadboard
  • A lot of wires

If you want many of the small parts (buttons, resistors, diodes, wires, etc.) all in one pack, starter kits like Arduino Starter Kit or Arduino Workshop Kit include a ton of components and a USB cable in one bundle.

Software Tools

  • Arduino IDE: free software to write and upload code to your board (from the official Arduino website).
  • Tinkercad Circuits: free online simulator to build and test your circuit virtually before you do it physically.

Tip: It’s easiest to start by building and testing your multimeter circuit in Tinkercad before you assemble it in real hardware. That way you can try out wiring and code without needing any soldering or physical setup first.
